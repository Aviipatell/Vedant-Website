30 Days. 30 Teams. 30 Posters — Designing a poster everyday for a month

In December 2019, I challenged myself to make 30 basketball posters (one for each team) in 30 days. This was to help improve my graphic design skills.

Role — Graphic Designer, Induvidual Project

Tools — Adobe Photoshop

Timeline — 4 weeks

Context

I’ve grown up watching basketball, and it has a pretty big thing in my life. Near the end of the 2019, I wanted to improve my visual design skills or just sports graphics skills in general so I decided to work on a poster everyday, one for each NBA team.

I tried to make sure that all the posters looked cohesive and was presented like a series. 30 days might not seem like too long compared to other 100 day or yearly challenges but in these 30 days, I was able to learn way more about sports graphics and improve my photoshop skills then I did over the past year.

Starting with shapes

To start off the series, I was going for a very simple and abstract look so I decided to play with shapes for the posters. I would try and incorporate different shapes of different colours to create dept and make them more vibrant. Although shapes were “easy” to work with, I learned that adding way too many, even if they are small, would ruin the poster. I had to really think about where I place each asset to make sure it does not throw the poster off. These were some of my favourites:

Adding pictures

I didn’t want to just keep working with shapes alone, at one point it was difficult to come up with different ideas so I decided to bring in images for some posters. Images related to either the player or team and made the design around that.

Mixing it up

I worked with shapes only posters, and I tried out images only posters, so now I wanted to tried mixing both of them! This became a bit more challenging because sometimes the graphics would be so detailed, it’d be pointless to add shapes, so in order to make both of them work together, I would try to clip the graphic into the shape to help make it more cohesive.

Reflection & Next Steps

This 30 day challenge was probably one of the best things I decided to, not only for my design career but also because of how much enjoyable it was to design these posters.

I definetely learned a lot from these posters, and one of them being that it’s okay to take a day break if you’re struggling to come up with something. At times, I would have no idea at all for a poster and would just stare at an empty canvas for hours. This was definetely the right way, and I learned that I should’ve just take a break and looked at it the next day to refresh my head.

Despite the challenge being mentally exhausting at times, it definetely is a way to keep me motivating, spending over 3-5 hours on each poster, stopping was definetely not an option. My photoshop skills have improved, and my vision for sports graphics has also improved immensely and I hope to use the skills I learned in this challenge in other projects I do.
