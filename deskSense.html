DeskSense — Designing a mobile health app for desk users.

DeskSense is an iOS health app designed to target professionals or students working at a desk for long periods of time to help with their physical and mental health.

Role — Product Designer, Induvidual Project

Tools — Figma

Timeline — 6 weeks

Problem

Designers have a passion problem. Many of us are workaholics and are constantly working at a desk or are interacting with our devices for long periods of time.

This constant interaction spirals into many health problems, mental and physical. Often these problems get overlooked, and are set aside because we are so immersed into our work. As a creative, I have experienced these issues and believe it is essential for us to take care of ourselves and work towards a solution. With the apps out right now, many are not targetted specifically for creatives, or people working at a desk. Many are also either focused on physical activity or mental activity based. This results in multiple apps being downloaded in order to meet the users needs.

Research

To further understand what health issues creatives face, I conducted a survey among other creatives. Although the questions might seem simple, they helped me gain a clear scope of the problem:

— What are some common health issues that you face as a creative?

The first question was constructed to get the respondent to start thinking about certain health issues they faced. With the responses I received, I decided to split these health issues into two: Mental and Physical.

Mental

  • Stress
  • Mental Exhaustion

Physical

  • Eye Strain
  • Back Pain
  • Dehydration / Malnourishment

— What are some things that you would like to do to improve your health?

— If you believe you have a healthy lifestyle, what are some things you do that allow you to have this kind of lifestyle?

After the respondents finish up answering the health issues they face, they are asked about what they wish to do or are doing to improve these issues. These were the most common responses:

  • To eat healthier
  • To exercising at least once a day, even if it's a light workout
  • Staying hydrated
  • Dehydration / Malnourishment

Goal

To help creatives improve their mental and physical health by providing different activities and reminders specifically meant for creatives all in one app.

KPI: Total reminders set for the user, number of activities saved and tried out.

Competitive Analysis

In order to get an understanding of other mobile health apps, I did a competitve analysis on a couple that stood out to me. These were two of the popular ones on the app store and similar to many others I found.

Calm

Strengths

  • Variety of mental exercises

Restrictions

  • Limited to only mental exercises
  • Many of the exercises are not accessible
Nike Training

Strengths

  • Limited to only mental exercises
  • Many of the exercises are not accessible

Restrictions

  • Limited to only physical exercises
  • Wide selection of exercises makes it difficult to navigate through the app

Wireframes

Initial Layout

My initial plan for the app was to just make it one screen for user convenience. I thought that having less screens would make it less confusing but when I looked into it, I was definetely wrong.

Although the one screen is simple, the user would have to keep clicking through each section going deeper into each category eventually making it harder to come to the original screen.

Strengths

  • Limited to only mental exercises
  • Many of the exercises are not accessible

Restrictions

  • Limited to only physical exercises
  • Wide selection of exercises makes it difficult to navigate through the app
Storyboard

After the initial thought I had for the app, I knew I had to make more wireframes and create a storyboard in order to make sure the users experience with the app was as simple as possible.

I decided to have four screens, the first one starting with the home screen which gives the user the ability to view their reminders for the day. The next screen is for mental activities where the user has access to various exercises that they can select. Same goes for the other two screens, one for physical activites and the other for tips.

Version 2

Strengths

  • Ability to navigate through app easily
  • Reminders are shown
  • Toggle button for the user to let the app know they're working

Restrictions

  • Shapes on cards make it too distracting
  • The helpful tips section is not neccessary and could be divided between the three screens

Final Version

View Figma Prototype

Changes made from version 2:

  • Reminders were moved up in the screen because they're more of a priority
  • Recent and saved activities tabs were added for convenience for the user
  • Shapes were removed and activities are easier to view
  • Bottom toolbar is changed to a snapchat inspired toolbar (with left and right swipe)
  • There reason for this was to make sure the user doesn't have to learn anything new for the app and can adapt to it easily

Reflection & Next Steps

Working on the DeskSense app has definitely been an effective learning exeprience for me. It allowed me to understand the importantance of storyboarding and putting myself in the users shoes.

At first, the amount of activities and health issues were overwhelming and I was confused on how I would organize everything but with the help of wireframing and user testing, I was able to break everything down, take out the unnecessary features and only keep the most important ones.

Although the first design process for the app is complete, there is still a lot more work to be done for the second version. My next steps would be to get users to test out the figma prototype and get feedback on how they like the app. I would also like the add a way to monetize the app, and how it can be able to gain passive income. For now, there is only an iOS version, which is why I would need to modify the app to work with other phones just as smootly because not everyone has an iphone. My final step would be to add more features that would cater the app towards designers.

This app has allowed me to realize to slow down my process and knowing that it’s not always about visuals and it’s important to think about how the user will interact with the app.
